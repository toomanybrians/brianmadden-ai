It's official! The leading AI computing using agent (CUA) now scores higher than humans in the OSWorld benchmark for real-world tasks in computer environments.

Simular Research Agent S3: 72.6%
Humans: 72.4%

Six months ago, the best AI agents scored 45%. A year ago it was 17%, and eighteen months ago, the best scored 12%.

I've written quite a bit on why computer-using agents will be huge for enterprise knowledge workers, and how it's inevitable that they'll use the same desktops, apps, and workspaces as human workers. The question was always "when," not "if."

To be clear, hitting human parity on a benchmark doesn't mean CUAs are ready to replace workers now. The benchmark tests raw UI execution‚Äîcan the agent click the right buttons, navigate menus, and complete task based on plain-language instructions? It doesn't test judgment, context, knowing why to do something, or handling the weird edge cases that fill every real workday.

But that's exactly the point. The "hands and eyes" part of computer work is pretty much solved. What remains is the orchestration & scaffolding that tells agents what to do, when, and with what guardrails.

For enterprise IT, this changes the conversation. It's no longer about whether AI can operate your legacy apps. It can. Now it's about where those agents will run, how you'll secure them, and who provides the oversight.
