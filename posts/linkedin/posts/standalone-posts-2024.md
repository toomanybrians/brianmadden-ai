Is Microsoft Copilot Vision the next version of Microsoft Recall?

No!

In fact they're two separate products with separate use cases.

(Following up a response I wrote about the latest episode of The Artificial Intelligence Podcast from Paul Roetzer and Mike Kaput ...)

Microsoft Recall is the "record everything I do on my computer via screenshots every few seconds" feature of Windows 11 that creeped everyone out a few months ago, and that Microsoft had to pause to fix security and defaults. It's now available again, and tied to Windows 11 + Copilot PCs.

Microsoft Copilot Vision is a separate product. For now it only works in the Edge browser, only for preapproved websites, and only when it's specifically invoked. Also, crucially, Vision does NOT create an archive of everything that's been seen in the browser. It only wakes up to view the screen when invoked, and it forgets/deletes what it saw when the chat ends.

So, these are two different products with different use cases.

Vision lets users have ephemeral chats about content in the browser with a cloud-based LLM/LVM. Recall is a screenshot archiving tool, with an AI-powered search capability that runs on device.

Over time I expect these capabilities will evolve and merge, and in the future every worker will have some kind of AI watching their screen and "assisting" them. But for now, they are both just very early previews of two very different products.
