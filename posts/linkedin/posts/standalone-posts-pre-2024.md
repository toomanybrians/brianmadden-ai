https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:ugcPost:6678671952678412288

Over the weekend, TechTarget shut down BrianMadden.com. Most of the thousands of stories and posts I wrote in my career are gone, replaced with 404 Page Not Found errors. They'll start to disappear from the search engines' results soon, and then the site will be nothing more than memories us old-timers carry around.

I still used the site quite a bit, mostly when looking into EUC history since BrianMadden.com was as close to the "paper of record" as anything this industry had—especially when you look at all the conversations and comments around the posts. But the world has moved on. The conversations are now on social media, not blogs, content is shorter, and business models are different. 

Funny though, I created a conference we did 20 times around the world, but what evidence is there of that? (Just memories!) I wrote over 2,000 articles, but what evidence is there of that? (memories!) This is the circle of life, but the sudden, "Hey the majority of your life's work just vanished from the universe over the weekend" is an unexpected Monday.

The site's legacy is not its content, but the community that formed around it. We all believed we could impact this industry by sharing what we know, and I'm happy that's living on today!

My eulogy for BrianMadden.com:

Why the Motorola Atrix sucked: My main complaint was that having the laptop-sized "dock" that didn't do anything was worthless. If you're carrying something the size of a laptop around, why not carry an actual laptop (ultrabook/netbook/etc) that could actually do useful work? They're the same price. A USB sync cable from your regular phone to your laptop *also* means you have everything with you, PLUS the option to use the laptop.
